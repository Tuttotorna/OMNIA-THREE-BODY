# Perturbation Regimes

## Purpose

This experiment measures how structural divergence changes as the initial perturbation increases.

The goal is not to predict exact future trajectories.

The goal is to measure:

```text
how long structural coherence survives
under controlled perturbation


---

Experiment

A reference three-body system is simulated.

Then the x-coordinate of the third body is perturbed by different values:

ε = 1e-06
ε = 1e-05
ε = 1e-04
ε = 1e-03
ε = 1e-02

For each perturbation, OMNIA measures:

TΔ — structural divergence time

Ω — residual structural coherence

IRI — accumulated irreversible divergence

Interpretation

The sweep shows a coherent structural pattern:

ε increases
↓
TΔ decreases
Ω decreases
IRI increases

Meaning:

larger initial perturbation
=
shorter coherence survival time
+
lower residual coherence
+
higher irreversible divergence


---

Regime structure

The observed behavior suggests three regimes.

1. Stable regime

For very small perturbations:

ε = 1e-06
ε = 1e-05

The system does not cross the divergence threshold within the simulated horizon.

In this experiment:

TΔ = -1

means:

no threshold crossing within 4000 steps


---

2. Transition regime

At:

ε = 1e-04

the system crosses the divergence threshold at:

TΔ = 1345

This indicates the first measured collapse of structural coherence within the simulation window.


---

3. Rapid collapse regime

For larger perturbations:

ε = 1e-03
ε = 1e-02

structural coherence collapses much earlier.

Measured values:

ε = 1e-03 → TΔ = 530
ε = 1e-02 → TΔ = 451

Canonical interpretation

This experiment does not prove a universal law.

It shows that OMNIA can measure a coherent perturbation-response structure in deterministic chaotic dynamics.

The important result is not that chaos exists.

The important result is that the loss of structural coherence can be measured parametrically.


---

Boundary

This experiment does not claim:

exact future-state prediction

solution of the three-body problem

new gravitational physics

universal instability law


It only measures structural divergence under controlled perturbation.
